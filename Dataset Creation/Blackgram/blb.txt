Core Information
Field
	Details
	Disease Name
	Bacterial Leaf Blight (BLB)
	Scientific Name
	Xanthomonas axonopodis pv. phaseoli
	Category
	Bacterial Disease
	Severity Index
	4/5 (High Economic Impact)
	Seasonal Patterns
	Peak infection during Kharif (Monsoon) and early Rabi (Winter) seasons, especially in high humidity conditions.
	Environmental Factors
	Prefers warm temperatures (25-35°C), high humidity (75-90%), and spreads rapidly in wet conditions or after heavy rainfall.
	Region
	Andhra Pradesh, Telangana
	Region-Specific Info
	Andhra Pradesh: High occurrences in Krishna, Guntur, Nellore. Telangana: Frequently reported in Warangal, Nizamabad, Karimnagar, especially in fields with excessive nitrogen fertilization.
	Symptoms & Visual Indicators
Early Stage (Initial Infection)
* Symptoms: Water-soaked spots on leaves, which later turn yellow.
* Visual Indicator: Small, irregular dark-green patches with yellow halos on young leaves.
Mid-Stage (Lesion Expansion)
* Symptoms: Spots enlarge into V-shaped lesions along leaf margins.
* Visual Indicator: Brown, necrotic lesions with a yellow border, leading to curling and drying of leaves.
Advanced Stage (Severe Infection)
* Symptoms: Leaves fall off, pods show bacterial oozing, and plant growth is stunted.
* Visual Indicator: Sticky bacterial exudates on stems and pod surfaces, turning black in humid conditions.
Prevention & Control
Cultural Practices
1. Use Disease-Free Seeds: Avoid planting seeds from infected crops.
2. Avoid Overhead Irrigation: Bacterial spread increases with splashing water.
3. Crop Rotation: Avoid continuous planting of Black Gram in infected fields.
4. Field Sanitation: Remove infected crop residues to reduce bacterial survival.
Biological Controls
1. Spray Pseudomonas fluorescens (5g/L water): Suppresses bacterial activity.
2. Apply Neem Oil Extracts (2%): Acts as a preventive measure.
3. Encourage Natural Antagonistic Bacteria like Bacillus subtilis: Reduces bacterial spread.
Chemical Controls
⚠️ Apply bactericides only when infection exceeds 10-15% of the field.

	What is Bacterial Leaf Blight?
	Bacterial Leaf Blight (Xanthomonas axonopodis pv. phaseoli) is a bacterial disease that causes leaf lesions, defoliation, and pod infections in Black Gram.
	How does Bacterial Leaf Blight spread?
	It spreads through rain splashes, infected seeds, and overhead irrigation.
	Can Bacterial Leaf Blight affect other crops?
	Yes, it also affects Green Gram, Soybean, and Cowpea.
	Does the bacteria survive in soil?
	Yes, it can persist in infected crop residues and contaminated seeds for several seasons.
	What are the symptoms of Bacterial Leaf Blight?
	Symptoms include water-soaked spots on leaves, V-shaped necrotic lesions, and bacterial oozing on pods.
	How do I identify Bacterial Leaf Blight early?
	Look for tiny dark-green spots with yellow halos on young leaves.
	Can fungal diseases cause similar symptoms?
	Yes, Cercospora Leaf Spot also causes leaf spots but its lesions have gray centers instead of bacterial exudates.
	What happens if Bacterial Leaf Blight is left untreated?
	Severe infections can cause 40-50% yield loss due to leaf drop and poor pod formation.
	When does Bacterial Leaf Blight occur?
	It is most common in monsoon and early winter seasons, especially in humid conditions (75-90% RH).
	Does rain increase Bacterial Leaf Blight?
	Yes, heavy rainfall promotes bacterial spread through splashing water droplets.
	Can dry weather stop Bacterial Leaf Blight?
	Yes, the disease slows down in hot, dry conditions but remains dormant until favorable conditions return.
	Which months are most at risk for Bacterial Leaf Blight?
	July-October during monsoon is the peak infection period for this disease.
