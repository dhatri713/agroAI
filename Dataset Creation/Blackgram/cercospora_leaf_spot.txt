Core Information
Field
	Details
	Disease Name
	Cercospora Leaf Spot
	Scientific Name
	Cercospora canescens
	Category
	Fungal Disease
	Severity Index
	3/5 (Moderate Economic Impact)
	Seasonal Patterns
	Peak infection during Kharif (Monsoon) and Rabi (Winter) seasons, especially after prolonged leaf wetness.
	Environmental Factors
	Prefers moderate temperatures (22-30°C), high humidity (75-90%), and spreads rapidly under rainy or wet conditions.
	Region
	Andhra Pradesh, Telangana
	Region-Specific Info
	Andhra Pradesh: High occurrences in Guntur, Krishna, Nellore. Telangana: Frequently reported in Nizamabad, Warangal, Medak, especially in areas with continuous Black Gram cultivation.
	Symptoms & Visual Indicators
Early Stage (Initial Infection)
* Symptoms: Small, circular, water-soaked spots appear on lower leaves.
* Visual Indicator: Tiny reddish-brown spots with yellow halos on leaf surfaces.
Mid-Stage (Lesion Expansion)
* Symptoms: Lesions enlarge, turn dark brown to gray, and spread across the leaf surface.
* Visual Indicator: Irregular grayish patches with dark brown edges, leading to reduced photosynthesis.
Advanced Stage (Severe Infection)
* Symptoms: Leaves dry out, curl, and drop prematurely, leading to 30-40% yield loss.
* Visual Indicator: Leaves appear scorched with multiple overlapping lesions; fungal spores may be visible under humid conditions.
Prevention & Control
Cultural Practices
1. Use Disease-Resistant Varieties: Growing resistant varieties reduces Cercospora infections.
2. Avoid Overhead Irrigation: Water on leaves promotes fungal spread.
3. Crop Rotation: Avoid continuous planting of Black Gram in the same field.
4. Sanitation Practices: Remove infected crop residues to reduce fungal spore survival.
Biological Controls
1. Spray Trichoderma viride (5g/L): Suppresses fungal spores.
2. Apply Neem Oil Extracts (2%): Acts as a natural fungicide.
3. Encourage Natural Antagonistic Fungi like Bacillus subtilis: Inhibits Cercospora growth.
Chemical Controls
⚠️ Apply fungicides only when infection spreads beyond 10-15% of the field.

	What is Cercospora Leaf Spot?
	Cercospora Leaf Spot is a fungal disease caused by Cercospora canescens, leading to leaf lesions and reduced yield in Black Gram.
	How does Cercospora Leaf Spot affect yield?
	Severe infections cause 30-40% yield loss due to premature leaf drop.
	Can Cercospora Leaf Spot spread to other crops?
	Yes, it also affects Green Gram and Cowpea.
	Does Cercospora remain in the soil?
	Yes, fungal spores can survive in infected crop residues for years.
	What are the symptoms of Cercospora Leaf Spot?
	Symptoms include small brown spots on leaves that grow into large gray lesions, leading to leaf drying and falling.
	How do I identify Cercospora early?
	Look for tiny reddish-brown spots with yellow halos on young leaves.
	Can other diseases cause similar spots?
	Yes, Bacterial Leaf Blight and Powdery Mildew also cause leaf spots but differ in lesion characteristics.
	What happens if Cercospora is left untreated?
	It spreads rapidly, causing severe defoliation and poor pod formation.
	When does Cercospora Leaf Spot occur?
	It is most common in monsoon and winter seasons, especially in humid conditions (75-90% RH).
	Does rain increase Cercospora infection?
	Yes, prolonged leaf wetness promotes fungal spore germination.
	Can Cercospora infect drought-stressed plants?
	No, it requires high humidity and moisture for infection.
	Which months are most at risk for Cercospora?
	July-October during monsoon is the peak infection period.
